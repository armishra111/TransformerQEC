# Publication readiness audit for a 1.3M physics-informed QEC transformer

**The current package is not top-venue ready.** The strongest realistic targets are an ML4PS / ML4PhysicalSciences-style workshop (best odds), followed by *Physical Review Research* or *Quantum* with a modest experimental upgrade. NeurIPS/ICML main track, PRX Quantum, and Nature are effectively closed for a 1.3M-parameter, phenomenological-noise, d≤7 decoder that is capacity-bound at d=7. Two developments have raised the bar since the project began: the **Nov 2024 publication of AlphaQubit** (Bausch et al., *Nature* 635, 834; arXiv:2310.05900), and the **December 2025 preprint of AlphaQubit-2** (Senior et al., arXiv:2512.07737) which explicitly uses RoPE on physical qubit xy coordinates — directly overlapping with the DIPE novelty claim. Below is a direct, section-by-section audit.

## Venue reality check in April 2026

The honest ranking of acceptance probability for the *current* results is **workshop ≫ PRR ≳ Quantum > PRX Quantum ≫ NeurIPS/ICML/ICLR main ≫ Nature**. Workshop venues (NeurIPS ML4PS, ICML AI4Science, NeurIPS QML) are the natural fit — they accept solid methodological work that isn't trying to move SOTA, and page limits (4–6 pages) match the scope of three architectural tweaks. *Physical Review Research* and *Quantum* (quantum-journal.org) are reachable with one circuit-level noise data point, a BP+OSD baseline, and a d=9 single-p run. *PRX Quantum* is borderline — it demands a "significant conceptual or practical advance" and benchmarks against AlphaQubit-class decoders; capacity failure at d=7 reads as a scaling pathology rather than a contribution. Main-track ML venues (NeurIPS/ICML/ICLR) want **ML novelty**, not a physics application; the architectural claims as currently framed would be dismissed as incremental RoPE/TTA tuning. *Nature* and *Nature Communications* would desk-reject: both AlphaQubit and the Willow paper (Acharya et al., *Nature* 638, 920; arXiv:2408.13687) set a circuit-level, experimental-data, d≥7 bar that this project does not clear.

On dual submission: **arXiv is fine for all venues**. NeurIPS/ICML/ICLR explicitly permit arXiv preprints but prohibit simultaneous submission to any archival peer-reviewed venue. The defensible path is **arXiv → ML workshop → expanded journal version at PRR or Quantum**; an ML main-track paper followed by a substantially extended journal version is also allowed but only if the journal version adds material beyond the conference proceedings. You cannot run a NeurIPS submission and a PRX Quantum submission in parallel on substantially the same manuscript.

Page norms: **NeurIPS 2025/2026 = 9 pages main + unlimited appendix**; ICML 2026 = 8 pages + unlimited; ICLR 2026 = 9 pages + 1 camera-ready. *Nature* requires a single integrated PDF ≤30 MB with ~50 main refs plus Extended Data and SI. *PRX Quantum* uses APS flexible Research Article length. *Quantum* has no length limits and is an arXiv overlay journal with ~13-week turnaround.

## The AlphaQubit gap is larger than it looks

AlphaQubit's confirmed specs matter because the project will be benchmarked against them. **AlphaQubit is 5.4M parameters total, shared across all code distances d=3–11**; the paper explicitly contrasts this with a 200M LSTM that failed at d=11 — *this is exactly the reference class the current 1.3M model's d=7 capacity failure will land in*. AlphaQubit uses a **per-stabilizer** representation (vector per stabilizer qubit), three Syndrome-Transformer layers per recurrent block combining attention with dilated 2D convolutions, and crucially trains on **SI1000 circuit-level noise + Pauli+ (leakage, crosstalk, soft readout) + real Sycamore data** — it never touches phenomenological noise. Its position encoding in the default per-distance model is a **learned index embedding**; the joint multi-distance variant uses a **learned relative positional embedding from 2D coordinates normalized to [−1,1]²** plus T5-style learned attention bias. At Pauli+ with soft inputs, AlphaQubit beats correlated MWPM by ~25–40% across d=3–11, largest gap at d=9.

**AlphaQubit-2 (arXiv:2512.07737, v2 March 2026)** is the direct threat to the DIPE framing. Quoted from the preprint: *"For spatial self-attention we use Rotational Position Encodings (RoPE) of the spatial coordinates of the physical qubits. Half of the channels use the x coordinate, half the y coordinate."* It trains to **surface d=23 and color d=27**, with the real-time distilled variant running **<1 μs/cycle up to surface d=11 and color d=9**. This preprint, if cited by reviewers, eliminates the primitive novelty of "RoPE on physical coordinates for QEC" entirely — AQ2 does essentially this, at vastly larger scale, with a 50:50 xy split rather than anisotropic.

Other relevant 2024–2026 comparators include **Transformer-QEC (arXiv:2311.16082)** which trained distance-transfer explicitly at d=3–11, the **Mamba state-space decoder (arXiv:2510.22724)** operating at O(d²) vs transformer O(d⁴) on Sycamore d=3/5, **Astra GNN (Maan & Paler, npj QI 11, 78, 2025; arXiv:2408.07038)** outperforming BP-OSD on surface and bivariate-bicycle codes, **Lange et al. GNN (PRR 7, 023181, 2025; arXiv:2307.01241)**, and **Varbanov et al. (PRR 7, 013029, 2025)** — a 25%-below-MWPM NN decoder on Sycamore. None of these used phenomenological noise at their primary evaluation point.

## Novelty claims under adversarial scrutiny

Each of the three architectural claims survives only in reframed form. Claiming primitive novelty on any of them invites a reviewer who knows the video-transformer and equivariance literature to reject.

**Distance-Invariant Position Encoding (DIPE)** is not a new primitive. It decomposes cleanly into two known ingredients: (i) using **absolute integer coordinates** for RoPE — which is exactly the mechanism in RoPE-ViT (Heo et al., arXiv:2403.13298, ECCV 2024) for resolution extrapolation; and (ii) **retuning the base frequency** so fixed coordinates retain identical angles under a scale change — which is the NTK-aware / YaRN / ABF trick (Peng et al., arXiv:2309.00071; Xiong et al., arXiv:2309.16039). AlphaQubit-2's RoPE-on-physical-coords further dilutes any primitive claim. **The defensible framing is not "new positional encoding" but "a specific inductive-bias recipe for zero/few-shot distance transfer in QEC decoders,"** with the contribution being (a) identifying physical-cell angle invariance as the right prior for surface-code decoding, (b) empirically demonstrating distance transfer from training distances to unseen distances, and (c) the specific tuned bases (100 spatial, 20 temporal) justified via lattice-spacing arguments. Expect reviewers to cite RoPE-ViT and YaRN as prior art; preempt this explicitly in the related work.

**The 3:1 anisotropic spatial:temporal split** sits in well-charted territory. **Factorized head-dimension allocation across space-time axes is standard in video transformers**: M-RoPE in Qwen2-VL (2024), VideoRoPE (Wei et al., arXiv:2502.05173, ICML 2025 Oral), HoPE, MRoPE-Interleave, and Multi-Head RoPE all sweep t:h:w allocation ratios as an ablation. The specific numerical ratio 3:1 does not appear to be named, but "low-frequency temporal allocation" in VideoRoPE is the operative prior art. The "non-Lorentzian phenomenological noise structure" physics framing is genuinely new — no video paper justifies its ratio by reference to a noise covariance structure — but the *mechanism* is off-the-shelf. Frame it as "a VideoRoPE-family allocation, selected for QEC by ablation, physically motivated by the unequal spatial–temporal correlation lengths of phenomenological noise," ablated against 1:1, 2:1, and 1:3. Under circuit-level SI1000, measurement-error asymmetry (5p vs 2p for measurement vs reset) actually breaks the temporal-symmetry argument, which is worth discussing as a subtle physics point.

**The D₄ × time-reversal TTA restricted to Z₂×Z₂ is the most defensible claim**, but it also requires reframing. The group-theoretic reasoning is correct: a 90° rotation on the rotated surface code checkerboard swaps X- and Z-stabilizer plaquettes, which for Z-memory without an X↔Z duality operator is not a symmetry; what survives is the Klein four-group {e, r₂, eT, r₂T}. **The END equivariant decoder (Egorov, Bondesan, Welling, arXiv:2304.07362, ICLR 2023)** already uses the full lattice automorphism group plus X↔Z duality for the *toric* code, and its 3D extension (Weißl & Egorov, arXiv:2409.04300, IEEE QCE 2025) does the same in 3D. **No 2023–2026 neural decoder paper explicitly builds D₄ or Z₂×Z₂ equivariance into the rotated planar surface code**, so this is a genuine open slot. However, TTA over a four-element finite group is 15-year-old computer-vision practice and reviewers will demand a built-in equivariance baseline (a weight-tied model or a G-CNN-style layer) to prove TTA is not just "cheap equivariance." One subtlety to preempt: under SI1000, measurement/reset asymmetry strictly breaks exact time-reversal, leaving only Z₂ ({e, r₂}) as an exact symmetry and T as approximate. Sell the paper as *"principled identification of the exact residual symmetry group for rotated-memory QEC and empirical quantification of the TTA-vs-equivariance tradeoff at this group size"* — that survives scrutiny.

## Missing experiments ranked by reviewer reject-risk

The MUST tier is what separates a workshop accept from a workshop reject; the SHOULD tier is what separates PRR/Quantum from a revise-and-resubmit; the NICE tier is what closes the gap to PRX Quantum.

**MUST-HAVE before any submission.** A **2³ ablation matrix** over {DIPE, anisotropy, TTA} at fixed (d=5, p=0.005) — without this, the architectural novelty claims cannot be evaluated; ~8 GPU-days. A **capacity scan** sweeping parameter counts {0.3M, 1M, 3M, 10M} × d∈{3,5,7} — this is the only experiment that tells reviewers whether the d=7 failure is an architectural ceiling or a compute budget limit; ~12 GPU-days. A **BP+OSD baseline** alongside MWPM (Roffe et al., arXiv:2005.07016) — ~1 CPU-week via the `ldpc`/`bposd` package, essentially free. **Multi-seed variance** (≥3 seeds on headline configs) — NeurIPS's paper checklist requires error bars in §7. **Latency table** for batch-1 inference on a specified GPU vs PyMatching v2 sparse blossom (Higgott & Gidney, *Quantum* 9, 1600, 2025) — negligible compute, ~1 day. A **single SI1000 circuit-level noise point** at one p — this changes the noise regime from "phenomenological-only" (effectively desk-reject at any physics venue post-AlphaQubit) to "phenomenological + circuit-level pilot"; ~1–2 weeks to build using `stim.NoiseModel.SI1000()` plus retraining (~1 week GPU).

**SHOULD-HAVE for competitive journal submission.** **Distance-transfer experiments** — train at d=5, evaluate zero-shot at d=3 and d=7; this is the *entire selling point* of DIPE and its absence is indefensible; ~4 GPU-days. Transformer-QEC (arXiv:2311.16082) established this paradigm and AlphaQubit Figure 4b does it. **p-transfer** (train single p, test across the full range, with an ECE calibration plot) — ~1 GPU-day. **Extend to d=9 at a single p** even if capacity-limited; this demonstrates the log-log slope persists past d=7 and breaks the "capacity-bound at d=7" narrative into "capacity-bound *at this parameter count* at d=7" — ~2 GPU-days per model size. **Ensemble study** N∈{1,5,20} following AlphaQubit — tests whether ensembling recovers the d=7 gap. Compare against a **neural baseline** (re-implement AlphaQubit's syndrome-transformer block or use Lange/Astra via their GitHub) — physics reviewers want ML-vs-ML, not just ML-vs-MWPM; ~1–2 GPU-weeks. **Built-in Z₂×Z₂ equivariance** baseline to compare against TTA — this is the experiment that elevates TTA from "computer-vision trick" to "principled design choice"; ~2 GPU-days. **TTA overhead-vs-accuracy curve** (1, 2, 4 augmentations) — negligible compute.

**NICE-TO-HAVE polish.** Fine-tuning on real **Sycamore d=3/5 public data** elevates the paper from ML-only to physics-credible; the Mamba decoder paper does this. Attention-map **interpretability** showing the model attends to stabilizer neighbors consistent with known matching edges. **Longer-cycle generalization** (train 25 → test 100–1000 cycles) — AlphaQubit shows this in Figure 5 and it is cheap given an already-trained model. An **anisotropy-vs-biased-noise experiment** would strengthen the "non-Lorentzian" physics framing by showing the 3:1 ratio is actually doing noise-structure-specific work.

Total compute estimate for MUST+SHOULD: roughly **80–120 GPU-days on a single modern accelerator (A100/H100)**, dominated by the capacity scan and SI1000 retraining. This is tractable on a single 4-GPU node over a month.

## Framing differences: ML venue vs physics venue

The same data should be written two ways. For an **ML venue** (NeurIPS/ICML workshop or main track), the abstract leads with *"We propose a factorized-RoPE transformer decoder with physically-motivated anisotropic channel allocation and a minimal-symmetry test-time averaging scheme. We demonstrate zero-shot distance transfer on rotated surface codes."* The body emphasizes ablation rigor, positional-encoding taxonomy, and connects to geometric-deep-learning literature. Figures lead with the ablation heatmap and a scaling plot. Baselines are other ML decoders; MWPM is one reference point among several. The reviewer bar is **methodological novelty and ablation clarity**.

For a **physics venue** (PRR, Quantum, PRX Quantum), the abstract leads with *"We train a compact transformer decoder that matches correlated matching at d=5 and maintains the theoretical (d+1)/2 sub-threshold log-log scaling under phenomenological noise, with implied threshold 3.2%±0.3% consistent with optimal decoding. The architectural innovations — RoPE on physical coordinates, anisotropic head allocation — yield zero-shot distance transfer from d=5 to d=3."* The body leads with the scaling plot (log-log LER vs p with (d+1)/2 slopes overlaid), threshold estimate with error bar, and LER comparison table. Appendix covers ML details. The reviewer bar is **physical soundness and scaling evidence**.

**Common desk-reject causes to avoid.** At ML venues: no ablations; single-seed results; no compute disclosure. At physics venues: phenomenological-only noise without any circuit-level validation; d≤7 only; no BP+OSD baseline; no threshold error bar; no comparison to at least one neural decoder besides MWPM; claiming "distance-invariant" without demonstrating distance transfer.

## Concrete action plan

The path that minimizes time-to-publication while maximizing venue quality:

**Weeks 1–3: close MUST items.** Run the 2³ ablation matrix, capacity scan, BP+OSD baseline, multi-seed variance, latency table. Freeze the paper at this point for **submission to NeurIPS 2026 ML4PS workshop or ICML 2026 AI4Science workshop** (typical deadlines mid-summer and spring respectively). This gets community feedback and a citable venue quickly.

**Weeks 4–10: close SHOULD items.** Build SI1000 circuit-level pipeline and retrain at d∈{3,5,7,9}. Run distance transfer, p-transfer, ensemble study, and a built-in equivariance baseline. Reimplement a small AlphaQubit-style block or run Lange's GNN as a neural baseline. At this point, **submit to Quantum journal or Physical Review Research** as the archival venue. Write with the physics framing.

**Weeks 11+: conditional escalation.** If the capacity scan reveals that a larger per-stabilizer-shared model (AQ-style weight sharing) reaches AlphaQubit-class performance at d=9+, and SI1000 results hold at d=9, *then* a PRX Quantum submission becomes realistic with a 3–4 month extension. Without both conditions, do not target PRX Quantum — the submission will be transferred to PRR anyway.

**Do not attempt** Nature, Nature Communications, or NeurIPS/ICML main track with the current scope even after all MUST+SHOULD experiments. The AlphaQubit and AlphaQubit-2 gap at those venues is structural: 5.4M–larger models, circuit-level + experimental data, d up to 23. Trying would burn 6+ months on desk rejection and leave the project scooped.

## What has actually been learned here

The project's genuine contribution is narrower than the stated novelty claims, but it is real. The **systematic identification of the Z₂×Z₂ residual symmetry for rotated Z-memory** is correct and unexploited in prior ML decoders. The **application of RoPE on integer stabilizer coordinates with distance-normalizing base frequencies** is a sensible inductive bias for distance transfer, even if its component operations are off-the-shelf from the video-transformer literature. The **capacity-bound failure at d=7** is not a bug to hide — it is data. A 1.3M-parameter non-per-stabilizer-shared architecture hitting a wall at d=7 in 2026, while AlphaQubit's 5.4M shared-weight architecture reaches d=11, is an empirically useful data point about **weight-sharing inductive bias vs raw capacity**, and a short *honest* section making this argument converts a weakness into a contribution. The project's log-log slopes within 5% of (d+1)/2 and R²>0.996 are genuinely tight scaling evidence — this should be the lead physics figure. Phenomenological-noise thresholds in the 2.8–3.8% range are consistent with the accepted literature band (2.9% MWPM per Wang-Harrington-Preskill 2003; 3.3% optimal via random-plaquette Z₂ gauge mapping), so the physics is sound.

**The central strategic choice is positioning.** Aim for a *principled, compact, well-ablated* decoder paper at a specialist venue (PRR, Quantum, workshop) on the current scope. Do not pitch an architectural revolution — the revolution happened at Google in November 2024 and December 2025. Pitch instead a tight study of inductive biases for distance-transferring decoders, with honest disclosure of the capacity ceiling and a clear roadmap to per-stabilizer weight sharing for future scaling. That paper is publishable within three months. The alternative — chasing Nature or NeurIPS main track — is not.
